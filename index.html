Visit Mantova

Enjoy arts and culture in this Italian gem of the Renaissance.

Guided Walking Tour

Explore the birthplace of Virgil and the setting for Verdi's “Rigoletto”. See historic monuments and marvel at the medieval palaces and Renaissance buildings of public squares.

Peschiera-Mantova Cycle Path

Explore Lower Garda by e-bike and escape the chaos of cities and mass tourism. Ride through the area in a sustainable, slow way, in the open air and completely in touch with nature.

Palazzo Ducale

In the historic center, with over 1000 rooms, it was the residence of the Bonacolsi family and then the Gonzagas when they took over in 1328.

Castel San Giorgio

Originally a fortress, it was converted into a residence by Ludovico I Gonzaga in the 15th century. All the Renaissance details are reminiscent of Florence.

Palazzo Te

For a taste of the Gonzaga rulers' opulent taste. Originally located outside the building wall, it embodied the il dolce far niente (the sweetness of doing nothing) ideology.

Duomo di Mantova

Also known as Cattedrale di San Pietro, the duomo is an ecletic mix of styles. The site was once occupied by a different building that burnt down in 984.
